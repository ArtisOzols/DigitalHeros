About our website

Cultural values are the basis of society and huge number of researches are aimed to analyze the population of different countries and regions by their social, political, economic, religious and cultural values. The data obtained make it possible to explain the actions and nature of people and societies, as well as to build theories about the similarities and differences between population groups. Accordingly, political scientists, sociologists, social psychologists, anthropologists, economists are extremely interested in collecting and analyzing this data.

Our website is created to show visually countries and their values, represent the data collected from WVS database in convenient digital format and to back it up with analysis which will be made with the help of Tableau visualizations.

Aims

Our team Digital Heroes is aimed to investigate difference in cultural values and compare countries in the Baltic States with closest countries. This project is set in order to collect the data about values in the countries of Baltic States and closest counties, to analyze the data as well as to make an analysis of how economic crisis and other significant factors have changes values over the time.

Mission

Our mission is to emphasize values in Latvia and other countries of Baltic region, compare values with other countries and to make an analysis of current observation. Accordingly, we are aimed to create a forecast of possible changes in values and upcoming changes.

Data visualizations

Value comparison in Baltic states through years

We visualized four values and how they change through years in Baltic states. We choose wave three with time period from 1995 till 1998, wave 6 with time period from 2010 till 2014 and wave 7 with time period from 2017 till 2020. We did not take wave 4 and wave 5 because there was no data about Baltic states. You can see that family has been very important in all time periods. Friends have been very important in wave 6, but in the rest waves respondents have answered that friends are rather important. Politics in all these waves are not important for respondents. And work has been very important in first two waves, but in last wave respondents have answered that work is rather important. We think it is related with fact that nowadays it is important to have work-life balance. Work-life balance is when person prioritizes the demands of work and demands of personal life. A good work-life balance is when you have less stress, lower risk of burnout and a great sense of well-being.

Economic situation impact on highest rated values

In here you can see how economic situation affects respondents' values in answer “very important”. We took data from the World Bank about Latvia’s economic situation from 1997 till 2019. We have 3 time periods with three data, but economic situation data for each year. To make this together we took one year from each time period. From wave 3 we took year 1997, from wave 6 year 2012 and from wave 7 year 2018. Then we took economic situation data from each of these years. So here you can see that in year 2012 there was one of the lowest points in economic situation. But at the same time in that year, we can see that family, friends and work is valued the highest. We can conclude that in the bad economic situation people in Baltic states value very high family, friends and work.

Changes of family value through years

In this map we visualized how value “family” change through years in Europe. We choose wave 3 and wave 7 and respondents answer “very important”. We took all Europe countries where the data was available. If you go to each red points, you will see how much precents of respondents have answered that family is very important to them.

Family value comparison

Here you can see the same data in map. The biggest squares show that in those countries more respondents answered that they value family “very important” in third wave. For example, in Slovakia 90,90 percent of respondents answered that family is very important, but in Finland 80,40 percent of respondents answered that family is very important. And if the color is darker that shows that in that country more respondents answered that they value “very important” in seventh wave. For example, Poland is very dark and 93,30 precents respondents have answered that family is “very important”, but Lithuania is very light and only 66,70 precents of respondents have answered that family is “very important”.
