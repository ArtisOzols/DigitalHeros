About our website

Cultural values are the basis of society and huge number of researches are aimed to analyze the population of different countries and regions by their political, economic and cultural values. The data obtained explain actions and nature of people and societies, as well as theories about the similarities and differences between population groups. Accordingly, political scientists, sociologists, social psychologists, anthropologists, economists are extremely interested in collecting and analyzing this data.

Our website is created to show visually countries and their values, represent the data collected from World Values Survey database in convenient digital format and to back it up with analysis which will be made with the help of Tableau visualizations.

Aims

Our team Digital Heroes is aimed to investigate difference in cultural values, to collect the data about countries in the Baltic State and closest counties as well as to make an analysis of how economic crisis and current crisis have changed values over the time.

Mission

Our mission is to emphasize values in Latvia and other countries of Baltic region, compare values with other countries. To represent changes that happened during current crisis.

What is Economic Crisis

…before we move to data analysis it is worth to mention one crucial thing that had a great impact to what changed our life values over the time.

  • Recession
  • Bankruptcy
  • Collapse
  • Decline
  • Downturn
  • Inflation
  • Slide
  • Slump
  • Stagnation

…all these words represent Crisis.

What is Economic Crisis? It is a breakdown of a national, regional, or territorial economy. It occurs at the onset of a severe version of an economic contraction, depression, or recession and can last any number of years depending on the severity of the circumstances. An economic collapse can happen rapidly due to an unexpected event, or it may be preceded by several events or signs pointing to fragility in the economy. There are many examples of national-level economic collapse throughout our history. Each economic collapse typically has its own circumstances and factors.

The economic crisis hit European Union countries since late 2007. It has triggered many unpleasant effects that are still being felt today. Faced with such a situation, the question arises as to whether the negative economic consequences of the crisis have impacted the quality of life and to what extent. There is no doubt that it has transformed the lives of many individuals and families, even in advanced countries, where millions of people fell, or are at risk of falling, into poverty and exclusion.

At the same time economic instability is also the result of negative occurrences beyond the economic sphere, i.e. in the area of values and culture.

Economic system was to help members of society maintain their position and all the related privileges: this was considered superior and provided more effective economic stimulus for trade than sheer economic gain. According to Polanyi (2001: 48): “… man’s economy, as a rule, is submerged in his social relationships. He does not act so as to safeguard his individual interest in the possession of material goods; he acts so as to safeguard his social standing, his social claims, his social assets” (Polanyi, 2001:48). Values played a fundamental role not only in the context of society but also in the context of economic system: economy embedded in human society was to establish such a system of allocation of wealth that would promote values of the society (ibid).

The main question for us during our project was – how did economic crisis influence life’s values?

Comparing the quality of life in European countries from years 2007 to 2011 allows us to analyze and quantify the impact of the economic crisis on people’s life values.

Data visualizations

Value comparison in Baltic states through years

We visualized four values and how they change through years in the Baltic states. We choose wave three with time period from 1995 till 1998, wave 6 with time period from 2010 till 2014 and wave 7 with time period from 2017 till 2020. We did not take wave 4 and wave 5 because there was no data about the Baltic states. You can see that family has been very important in all time periods. Friends have been very important in wave 6, but in the rest waves respondents have answered that friends are rather important. Politics in all these waves are not important for respondents. And work has been very important in first two waves, but in last wave respondents have answered that work is rather important. We think it is related with fact that nowadays it is important to have work-life balance. Work-life balance is when person prioritizes the demands of work and demands of personal life. A good work-life balance is when you have less stress, lower risk of burnout and a great sense of well-being.

Economic situation impact on highest rated values

In here you can see how economic situation affects respondents' values in answer “very important”. We took data from the World Bank about Latvia’s economic situation from 1997 till 2019. We have 3 time periods with three data, but economic situation data for each year. To make this together we took one year from each time period. From wave 3 we took year 1997, from wave 6 year 2012 and from wave 7 year 2018. Then we took economic situation data from each of these years. So here you can see that in year 2012 there was one of the lowest points in economic situation. But at the same time in that year, we can see that family, friends and work is valued the highest. We can conclude that in the bad economic situation people in the Baltic states value very high family, friends and work.

Changes of family value through years

In this map we visualized how value “family” change through years in Europe. We choose wave 3 and wave 7 and respondents answer “very important”. We took all Europe countries where the data was available. If you go to each red points, you will see how much precents of respondents have answered that family is very important to them.

Family value comparison

Here you can see the same data from map. The biggest squares show that in those countries more respondents answered that they value family is “very important” in third wave. For example, in Slovakia 90,90 percent of respondents answered that family is very important, but in Finland 80,40 percent of respondents answered that family is very important. And if the color is darker that shows that in that country more respondents answered that they value family as “very important” in seventh wave. For example, Poland is very dark and 93,30 precents respondents have answered that family is “very important”, but Lithuania is very light and only 66,70 precents of respondents have answered that family is “very important”.

Conclusions

As expected, the conclusions to emerge from our analysis to significant changes in both the individual as well as relative situation in European countries is an evidence that there has been a substantial decline in citizens’ quality of life. However, family, friends, work and politics are important parts of life. We wanted to show how people value these things in other countries and how their opinion change through years. This project could be useful to show people how economic situation impacts our values and that in the dark times people are searching for comfort and want to be surrounded by family and friends, and at the same time evaluate the importance of work. Values always played a fundamental role not only in the context of society but also in the context of economic system.
