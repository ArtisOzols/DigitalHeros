Our group Digital Heroes performed the analysis of the World Values Survey Association dataset.

We have chosen this topic to compare the Baltic States values to the values of other closest countries and see how they are different or similar; to analyze how the values have changed over different periods of time, as well as to analyze how the economic crisis and other major factors have changed our values.

About our website

Cultural values are the basis of society and a huge number of researches are aimed to analyze the population of different countries and regions by their political, economic, and cultural values. The data obtained explain the actions and nature of people and societies, as well as theories about the similarities and differences between population groups. Political scientists, sociologists, social psychologists, anthropologists, economists are extremely interested in collecting and analyzing this data.

Our website is created to show visually countries and their values, represent the data collected from the World Values Survey database in convenient digital format, and back it up with analysis which will be made with the help of Tableau visualizations.

Aims

Our team Digital Heroes is aimed to investigate the difference in cultural values, to collect the data about countries in the Baltic State and closest counties as well as to analyze how economic crisis and current crisis have changed values over time.

The tasks of our research

  • To compare the information from different periods of time;

  • To represent data in three different ways;

  • To use digital tools to analyze and predict values in the Baltic States and other countries.

Mission

Our mission is to emphasize values in Latvia and other countries of the Baltic region, compare values with other countries, and make an analysis of current observation. Furthermore, we are aimed to create a forecast of possible changes in values and upcoming changes.

What is Economic Crisis

…before we move to data analysis it is worth mentioning one crucial thing that had a great impact on what changed our life values over time.

  • Recession
  • Bankruptcy
  • Collapse
  • Decline
  • Downturn
  • Inflation
  • Slide
  • Slump
  • Stagnation

…all these words represent Crisis.

What is Economic Crisis? It is a breakdown of a national, regional, or territorial economy. It occurs at the onset of a severe version of an economic contraction, depression, or recession and can last any number of years depending on the severity of the circumstances. An economic collapse can happen rapidly due to an unexpected event, or it may be preceded by several events or signs pointing to the fragility in the economy. There are many examples of national-level economic collapse throughout our history. Each economic collapse typically has its circumstances and factors.

The economic crisis hit European Union countries since late 2007. It has triggered many unpleasant effects that are still being felt today. Faced with such a situation, the question arises as to whether the negative economic consequences of the crisis have impacted our values and to what extent. There is no doubt that it has transformed the lives of many individuals and families, even in advanced countries. At the same time, economic instability is also the result of negative occurrences beyond the economic sphere, i.e. in the area of values.

The main question for us during our project was – how did the economic crisis influence our life’s values? Comparing the European countries from the years 1997, 2012, and 2018 allows us to analyze and quantify the impact of the economic crisis on people’s life values. Our group have chosen four values for investigation: family, friends, work, and politics.

Data visualizations

Let’s take a look at four values and how they change through the years in the Baltic states. We choose wave three with time period from 1995 till 1998, wave 6 with time period from 2010 till 2014, and wave 7 with time period from 2017 till 2020. We did not take wave 4 and wave 5 because there was no data about the Baltic states.

Value comparison in Baltic states through years

You can see that family has been very important in all periods. Friends have been very important in wave 6, but in the rest waves, respondents have answered that friends are rather important. Politics in all these waves are not important for respondents. And work has been very important in the first two waves, but in the last wave, respondents have answered that work is rather important.
We think that observed results are related to the fact that nowadays it is important to have a work-life balance. Work-life balance is when a person prioritizes the demands of work and the demands of personal life. A good work-life balance is when you have less stress, a lower risk of burnout, and a great sense of well-being [1].

Economic situation impact on highest rated values

Here you can see how the economic situation affects respondents’ values in answer “very important”. We took data from the World Bank about Latvia’s economic situation from 1997 till 2019. We have 3 time periods with three data sets, and economic situation data for each year. We combined it all and took one year from each time period. From wave 3 we took the year 1997, from wave 6 the year 2012, and from wave 7 the year 2018. Then we took economic situation data from each of these years. So here you can see that in the year 2012 there was one of the lowest points in the economic situation. But at the same time in that year, we can see that family, friends, and work are valued the highest. We can conclude that in the bad economic situation people in the Baltic states value very high family, friends, and work [2].

Changes of family value through years

In this map, we visualized how value “family” changes through the years in Europe. We choose wave 3 and wave 7 and respondents answer “very important”. We took all European countries where the data was available. If you go to each redpoint, you will see how many presents of respondents have answered that family is very important to them [3].

Family value comparison

Here you can see the same data from the map. The biggest squares show that in those countries more respondents answered that they value family as “very important” in the third wave. For example, in Slovakia, 90,90 percent of respondents answered that family is very important, but in Finland, 80,40 percent of respondents answered that family is very important. And if the color is darker, that shows that in that country’s more respondents answered that they value family as “very important” in the seventh wave. For example, Poland is very dark, and 93,30 percent of respondents have answered that family is “very important”, but Lithuania is very light, and only 66,70 percent of respondents have answered that family is “very important” [4].

Conclusions

The evidence of the impact that family has on the life of an individual has been actual for the researchers throughout decades. Thus, according to the researcher’s family and parents play a major role in the social and academic development of an individual during childhood. Additionally, several variables determine the impact of family and home environment, these are social and economic, level of parental education, family size, etc. However, the involvement of family in the development of an individual during childhood is crucial in overcoming different external factors in adulthood for example crisis. Therefore, the aim of parents and family overall is to offer encouragement and support as much as possible according to the individual’s needs.

In adolescence, the brain becomes as sensitive to social signals and connections as it will ever be, that is why young people are usually highly interested in social activity. As people grow and reach adulthood, start working, create families and have children it becomes harder to spend time with friends and mates. Accordingly in the elderly age, people tend to have more time again and can come back to friends, since jobs are less demanding, and kids are grown. As every person experiences these transitions in life and changes in the value of friendships it is important to note that friendship is usually a lifelong endeavor.

The importance of politics in an individual’s life is hard to deny since you have to know what is happening on around you. As the political decisions made by people affect not only people in one’s country but have a global impact. A lot of people see politics as governmental issues and those laws are broken this is not a rare thing, but it's much more complicated than that. Every rights violation will have its consequences. That is why people need to know the basic political situation, their rights, and laws. Similarly, an opportunity and will to express opinion and vote creates the situation and significantly impacts day to day life of people. Some ideas might be devastating for certain people and some might play a major role in the development of humanity.

The working status and position in modern societies is the result of a long historical process. It is not only a fundamental aspect of reinforcing an individual’s sense of fulfillment, usefulness, and belonging, but also the key ingredient of financial position. Work is also at the heart of several other dimensions, namely its role as a socialization mechanism, a source of social exchanges, and individual identity. Thus, work can be considered as a crucial part of social organization, but also, to a large extent, as an important component of the existential organization of individuals. It is a fundamental characteristic of many aspects of social inclusion such as health, housing, and people-to-people networks.
About the Baltic States
As expected, the conclusions to emerge from our analysis to significant changes in both the individual as well as the relative situation in European countries is the evidence that there has been a substantial decline in citizens’ quality of life. However, such values as family, friends, work, and politics are important parts of life. We wanted to show how people value these things in other countries and how their opinions change through the years. We wanted to compare Baltic states’ data with other countries since almost all of us live here and for us, it is important to see how happy we are with our life here.
Furthermore, it is important to see how the Baltic states’ values change through time because we can predict how these values will change based on certain circumstances. This project could be useful to show people how the economic situation impacts our values and that in the dark times people are searching for comfort and want to be surrounded by family and friends, and at the same time evaluate the importance of work. Values always played a fundamental role not only in the context of society but also in the context of an economic system.

Data Set

The represented values dataset is taken from World Values Survey Association. The World Values Survey (www.worldvaluessurvey.org) is a global network of social scientists studying changing values and their impact on social and political life, led by an international team of scholars, with the WVS association and secretariat headquartered in Stockholm, Sweden. In this survey are more than 300 questions about social values, attitudes, stereotypes, happiness, well-being, economic values, migration, etc. We chose to research social values and specific values as family, friends, work, and politics. We believe that these values are one of the most important values for every person and we wanted to look at how important these values are to the Baltic states’ population and to the rest of Europe.
